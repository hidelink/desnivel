Some routes are meant to be run, and some are meant to be walked slowly, on purpose, because the point isn't getting there fast but getting there well. This traverse along the Iztaccíhuatl ridge is the second kind.

The first day is all climbing: from La Joyita the forest falls away little by little until the terrain opens up completely and there's nothing left between you and the mountain. Reaching the refuge near Las Rodillas, already in the dark and with the cold finding every gap in your layers, feels like an achievement in itself — even before reaching the high point.

The second morning always brings the same question: does the weather allow continuing? When the answer is yes, the descent toward Los Portillos is a gift — tired legs, a full view of Popocatépetl, and the feeling of having truly slept, if only for one night, inside the mountain.
