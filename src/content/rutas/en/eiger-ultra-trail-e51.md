Some routes get planned months in advance; others just show up in front of you one day, boots already on. This one was the second kind: an ultra-distance route crossing the Bernese Oberland end to end, with the kind of elevation gain that doesn't let you lie to yourself about your fitness.

Leaving Grindelwald is deceptive. The village is still asleep, the air is cold, and the trail drops gently before it starts climbing in earnest. It's easy to go out fast — too fast — when you don't yet know what's coming.

What's coming is a long ridge that seems to never end. Climbs of over a thousand meters followed by technical descents on loose rock, the Eiger appearing and disappearing behind clouds rolling up from the valley by mid-morning. That's the stretch where everyone's race gets decided — not at the start, not in the final sprint.
